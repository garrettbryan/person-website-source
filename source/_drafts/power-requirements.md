I need 273 MJ delivered to the 9% efficient OSMF every 30 minutes for the OSMF to melt an ideal $latex 8000 cm^3$ pour. The power requirement works out to be: $latex P_{total} = U_{pour} \\times (\\frac{2 pours}{hour}) \\times (\\frac{1 hour}{3600 sec})$ Using [273 MJ as the energy required per pour](http://outlyingoutlier.com/2014/04/16/furnace-efficency/): $latex P_{total} = (\\frac{273 MJ}{pour}) \\times (\\frac{2 pours}{hour}) \\times (\\frac{1 hour}{3600 sec}) = 151.\\overline{6}kW \\approx 152kW $ This is a huge power requirement for a melting furnace designed for the maker community; however, the benefits outweigh the costs. The OSMF is designed for one off prototypes and very small runs, to maximize safety by separating the hot crucible from the operator, and to minimize other costs by using local building materials.
